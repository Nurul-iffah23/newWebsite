Web Of Life

The "Web of Life" shows how all living things in an ecosystem are connected and depend on each other. In a lake, this means that plants, animals, microorganisms, and water work together to keep the ecosystem healthy.

Resilience in a lake means its ability to bounce back from problems, like pollution or climate change. A lake is resilient when it can adapt to changes and continue to support life.

For example, plants in the lake help keep the water clean and provide shelter for fish, while fish and microbes help maintain balance.

When a lake is resilient, it can recover from disturbances and keep providing important services like clean water and habitat for wildlife.
