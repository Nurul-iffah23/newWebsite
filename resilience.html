Resilience of Ecological Systems in Lakes

What is Resilience of Ecological Systems in Lakes?

The resilience of an ecological system, such as a lake, refers to its ability to recover from disturbances and maintain essential ecological functions.

Lakes are complex ecosystems that rely on the interactions between various biotic and abiotic components, such as water, plants, animals, and microorganisms.

Key Factors
Biodiversity
Ensures ecological balance and adaptability
Water Quality
Supports healthy aquatic life and ecosystem stability
Natural Recovery Processes
Ability to recover naturally from disturbances
External Stressors
Pollution, invasive species, climate change
Human Intervention
Management, conservation, sustainable practices
